The ASL Translator was trained on the YOLOv8 model. We used a dataset of around 1700 images. The dataset was from Roboflow and was uploaded by David Lee, who took the photos. Approximately 80% of the data was used for training and the rest for testing and validation. We created this website, trained the model, and hosted the model during the Drexel CCI Data Science camp.

We aim to make learning sign language more accessible to people. For free. Anytime. Anywhere.

During our research of potential ideas, we came across some striking statistics regarding the population of deal people and people who know ASL. According to the 2021 American Community Survey (ACS), about 11 million individuals in the United States are deaf or hard of hearing, however only around half a million Americans use ASL as their native language. This sparked the creation of ASL Translator.
